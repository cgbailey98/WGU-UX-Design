About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island located in the Pacific Ocean with quite a diverse terrain which includes sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, a beautiful rainforest, and a mountainous interior with a small, active volcano. The population of Taniti is approximately 20,000, with its economy dominated primarily by fishing and agriculture. However, recently there has been quite an increase in tourism.

General Information for Tourists

Power outlets are 120 volts, which are the same as the outlets in the United States. Alcohol sales are restricted between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, but not strictly enforced. Over the years, and especially with the younger generations, English is becoming more common as the spoken language. However, in the more rural areas and by older Tanitians, the native language is more common. In Taniti, there is one hospital and several clincics. As the rise of tourism increases, petty crime is also becoming more common. The country also observes many national holidays, which in some cases can affect the operation of attractions and restaurants. The currency used in Taniti is the U.S. dollar, but euros and yen are accepted as well.
